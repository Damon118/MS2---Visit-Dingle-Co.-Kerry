Places to see

Killarney

Killarney is a bustling town on the shores of Lough Leane in southwest Kerry. The town boasts numerous historical attractions, such as Ross Castle, and is home to the world-famous Lakes of Killarney and Killarney National Park.

View on map
Dingle

Dingle is a small port town in southwest Kerry, known for its rugged scenery, trails and sandy beaches. Dingle is famed for its authentic pubs, welcoming people, spectacular coastline and is a popular destination on the Wild Atlantic Way.

View on map
Tralee

Tralee is a town in County Kerry, in southwestern Ireland. It’s most known for hosting the world-renowned the Rose of Tralee International Festival annually. The Kerry County Museum and the National Folk Theatre of Ireland are also home to Tralee.

View on map
Listowel

Listowel is a heritage market town in Kerry and home to Ireland's oldest literary festival. The Listowel Food Fair runs every November and promotes local artisan food products and attracts food lovers from around the globe.

Things to do

Hillwalking

The Kerry Way is undoubtedley the most spectacular hill walk in Ireland. The trail is 215km long is the best way to experience the awe-inspiring landscape and scenery the county has to offer. The route progresses through a variety of different landscapes, historical sites and experiences, giving the walker the complete Kerry experience!

Cycling

The Ring of Kerry is the most well-renowned and scenic cycle route in Ireland. It has gained popularity due to the annual and charitable Ring of Kerry event hosted on the first Saturday of July each year. This event brings together thousands of cyclists from around the globe with varying abilities who come to enjoy the scenery, take part in a challenge and participate in aid of a good cause!

Swimming

With it's glorious coastline and beaches, swimming and Kerry are a match made in heaven! Kells Bay is a charming harbour with a stunning beach and is located just off the Ring of Kerry. Dingle bay offered the opportunity to swim with the legendary Fungi the dolphin. Valentia Island harbors a unique naturally occuring rock pool that is big enough to swim in.

Camping

Wave Crest Camping Site is a prominent and family-run camp site in Caherdaniel on the Ring of Kerry. This scenic waterfront site with panoramic sea and cliff views offers the perfect base for a refreshing and reinvigoating holiday. Mannix Point Camping Site is situated on the Gulf Stream coast in South-West Kerry on The Wild Atlantic Way and is the top-rated camping spot in the county.
